First off, you will need:
1) DOA3 and the June 2002 OXM disc (see the booster costumes at Doa3 Gaiden)
2) An Xbox which you are willing to tamper with (thus voiding the warranty)
3) Torx screwdriver bits for opening the Xbox (they're six-point star shaped. Or try a small flathead)
4) A PC running Win2k/XP you can connect the Xbox hard drive to
5) Toogg's Driver v0.5, mirrored here: HDD_Driver.zip (33k). Required DLLs: dlls.zip (850k)/dlls.rar (650k)

However, with the recent advent of modchips, it is very likely that a homebrew utility will allow us to access the HD over a LAN. For more on modchips, click here.

Step 1: Install the booster pack
The booster model files must be installed onto the Xbox hard drive in order for you to access them.

Step 2: Connect the Xbox HD to your PC.
This part is not as easy as you might think. The Xbox HD is password protected, so unless you have an expensive logic analyzer, you'll just have to pull a swap trick!

With the Xbox open, make sure the DVD drive is empty and turn it on. The Xbox will unlock the HD and start it up. Once you're in the dashboard, unplug the IDE cable from the Xbox HD. Do NOT turn off the Xbox, as the drive must remain powered.

This may scare you! From my understanding, IDE cables are pretty benign, and I have done this countless times and know others who have as well, without any negative repercussions. However, I reiterate; Anything you do to your Xbox or PC is AT YOUR OWN RISK.

Then either: Power up your PC, press pause before it scans for IDE devices, connect the Xbox HD to your PC, and hit 'enter' to unpause. It may take more time then normal to boot your computer, but be patient.

Or: If you're running WinXP, you can perform the IDE cable swap at anytime after booting your OS. After swapping the drive, go to the Device Manager, right click on the IDE channel the HD is connected to (in the IDE ATA/ATAPI controllers tree), and "Scan for new devices". I'm not sure if this is available in Win2k.

For more information on performing the swap trick, see XboxHacker.Net

Step 3: Dump the model files to your PC's hard drive
Once the HD is detected (it will not show up in "My Computer"), use Toogg's utility to access the hard drive.

A few things to keep in mind while using Toogg's Driver v0.5; Read the included document! Back up your hard drive from the Tools menu before going any further! Right click on the [+] to open directories. Left click on the folder/file to get the dump/insert menu. Double clicking on folders with subdirectories will crash Driver. Driver v0.5 also has problems deleting and overwriting files. If you screw up, it will quite likely result in a costume choice that will crash your Xbox! Also, it will ask for confirmation on overwrite.. if you say "no", it will not close the file, and so the file cannot be opened again until Driver is restarted.

You will find the files at "User Save\TDATA\54430001". Left click on each file and "dump" it to one of your PC's HDs. You will also find DOA3SAVE.DAT here.

Each model has a *.cat and *.xpr file. As an example, here are the names of Ayane's booster model files for her two costumes (really just one with a color alternate):
ayaA0.cat / ayaA0.xpr
ayaA1.cat / ayaA1.xpr

While most model files can be identified by the first three letters of the characters' names, these files defy such easy categorization:
kor = Hitomi nin = Hayate sui = Brad gok = Christie
bay = Leon sam = Bayman hik = Helena

Step 4: Re-insert the renamed model files onto the Xbox HD
Re-insert the files into the model directory on your Xbox hard drive with filenames to match the desired fighting style.

I have tested filenames from "???A0" to "???C9" (not case sensitive), and although I would imagine it goes on from there, each character has only room for 8 or 9 costumes, depending on the character. The filenames do not have to be consecutive.

So, for example, inserting "kasA0.*" as "ayab1.*" would result in an Ayane model selectable from Kasumi's costume list in the first free slot available, as depicted here:

With Toogg's Driver 0.5, you must insert files one at a time. I've found that using the line "copy %1.* insert\%2.*" in a batch file to create a group of models to be inserted was a lot easier then renaming them upon insertion. Hopefully in a future release Toogg will add a function to insert whole directories.

Step 5: Reconnect the Xbox HD to the Xbox
Reconnect the Xbox HD, load up DOA3, and enjoy! Oh and be sure to email me and tell me how it turned out!

NOTE: If you screwed up and are left with costume choices that crash your Xbox, all is not lost! You can delete Doa3 all the data from your Xbox via the Dashboard or you can rename the offending models to something harmless and try deleting them (I've had very limited success deleting files with Toogg's util).

DOA3 Model Editing

I originally started playing with the DOA3 model files in the hope that they could be opened up in a retail model editor, thus opening the door to modelers around the world to add their own flavor to DOA3. This will soon become a reality, and you will see it first here on my website. Non-believers, be prepared to see the light...

This is what we know about the XPR files:

XPR is a Pack Resource File. It simply holds any graphic (or other) resources XBox apps require. It can store all D3D Resource Types (Textures, Vertex Buffers, Index Buffers, ...) and "UserData", which is just about anything. What appears to be the actual model file was dumped in as a "UserData" chunk. The DOA model also has Vertex Buffers and Textures, in it. The textures are in a D3D compression format.

Defenderz has spent a considerable amount of his personal time working on this project. Using his superior skills, he has ripped all the non-alpha (non-transparent) textures out of the Leifang leather dress model.
