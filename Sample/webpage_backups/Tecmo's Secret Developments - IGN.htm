Tecmo's Secret Developments

Tecmo has changed directions and looks to develop at least two games for 1998

With many NES and Super NES titles in its back pocket, Tecmo, one of Nintendo's older software partners, will produce games for Nintendo 64.

Linda Shannon, marketing coordinator for Tecmo North America, told N64.com, "we do have games in development for Nintendo 64." Shannon declined to say anything else other than that the games would arrive in late 1997 or possibly early 1998, and that, "Tecmo Bowl [64] is a possibility."

In previous conversations with Tecmo, the company did hint at a sequel to the Super NES football game for Nintendo 64, but didn't point to any other games. Of course, that was in early in the year, and many companies have changed their minds, having seen Nintendo's massive penetration rate. Tecmo seems to be one of these companies.

Best known for its football games on Super NES, the Tecmo Bowl series reached three on system despite distribution problems. Tecmo also created Ninja Gaiden, a side-scrolling action game with Ninja fighters, that reached number three in the series on the NES, and then was grouped and ported as one product on Super NES. It also created Super Basketball among other titles.

Tecmo is more recently known for its impressive Model 2 licensed fighting game, Dead or Alive, which is porting to Sega Saturn in either August or September. Although an agreement with Sega restricted the port of this game on any other system, the agreement may have loosened. There has been some banter from both Tecmo and Acclaim of the possible port of this game to PlayStation and Nintendo 64 as well. Mike Meyers of Acclaim told N64.com that Acclaim has looked into bringing that game to N64 in 1998, but he could not confirm either.
